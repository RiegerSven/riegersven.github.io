Abstract

Random-Coefficient Model I

The Random-Coefficient Model (also called Random-Intercept + Random-Slope Model) is a model cluster-varying level-1 predictor.

Level-specific equations

\[ \begin{aligned} \text{Level 1: } & Y_{ij} = \beta_{0j} + \beta_{1j}X_{ij} + r_{ij} \\ \text{Level 2: } & \beta_{0j} = \gamma_{00} + u_{0j} \\ & \beta_{1j} = \gamma_{10} + u_{1j} \end{aligned} \]

  • Combined equation: Substituting the level 2 equations into the level 1 equation yields the combined model equation:

\[ Y_{ij} = \gamma_{00} + u_{0j} + \gamma_{10}X_{ij} + u_{1j}X_{ij} + r_{ij} \qquad(1)\]

Random-Coefficient Model II

  • The following parameters have to estimated:
    • Fixed effects: \(\gamma_{00}\), \(\gamma_{10}\)
    • Random effects: \(VAR(u_{0j})\), \(VAR(u_{1j})\), \((RES)VAR(r_{ij})\)
    • Covariance: \(COV(u_{0j}, u_{1j})\)
  • Represent the level-2 random effects as a variance-covariance matrix:

\[T = \Sigma_{u} = VAR \begin{bmatrix} u_{0j} \\ u_{1j} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \tau_{00} & \tau_{01} \\ \tau_{10} & \tau_{11} \end{bmatrix} \]
